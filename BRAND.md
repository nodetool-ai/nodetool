# NodeTool Brand DNA

*Last updated: May 8, 2026*

## 1. Purpose

**To keep creative AI in the hands of the people making the work.**

We exist because the tools creatives use to make AI-assisted work are quietly being consolidated, rented, and locked down. Models live on someone else's servers. Workflows live in someone else's database. Roadmaps belong to whoever just got acquired. We think that's the wrong direction for a craft-driven discipline, and NodeTool is our answer.

## 2. Belief

**Creative tools shouldn't lock you into someone else's pricing, model roster, or roadmap.**

The industry believes the future of creative AI is a cloud platform with a curated model marketplace and a credit system. We believe the future is a workspace that talks to whatever models you choose, charges you what the providers charge, and outlives whoever built it.

Corollaries we'll defend:
- The best model for the job changes every month — your tool shouldn't slow that down.
- You should pay providers directly, at provider prices.
- Your workflows, files, and keys belong to you — running locally is one way to guarantee that, calling APIs with your own keys is another.
- Open source isn't a license decision. It's the only way the sentences above stay true.

## 3. Audience

**Core (who we build for first):** Independent generative artists, motion designers, AI-native illustrators, technical art directors, ComfyUI power users frustrated with the UX, and Weavy users looking for somewhere to go after the Figma acquisition. People who already know what Flux, Wan, Seedance, and ControlNet are, and who care about vendor neutrality and cost transparency.

**Adjacent (who we grow into):** Small creative studios, brand and design teams, post-production shops, creative technologists at agencies. People who need their team to use AI workflows without each person becoming a ComfyUI expert.

**Future (who we design toward but don't market to yet):** In-house creative ops at regulated industries — pharma, finance, government, defense — where "creative AI but our data can't leave the building" is currently an unsolved problem.

## 4. Category

**The open creative AI workspace.**

Not a workflow builder. Not an agent framework. Not a ComfyUI alternative. A workspace — the place creative work lives, gets iterated, and ships from. Open, because that's the strategic and ethical position. Creative, because that's the wedge and the audience. Workspace, because it's where work happens, not where work gets configured.

We are explicitly not in the "AI orchestration" category, the "LLM agent platform" category, or the "no-code automation" category — even though the underlying tech overlaps. Those categories belong to other companies and we don't want to win them.

## 5. What we are not

A brand is also defined by what it refuses to become. NodeTool is not, and will not be:

- **A model host.** We don't run inference for you on our servers and resell it. You bring keys to the providers you choose.
- **A credit system.** No proprietary tokens, no minimum top-up, no markup on model calls.
- **Local-only.** Local inference is supported. It's not required, and it's not always the right answer.
- **A closed platform.** Open source, runnable anywhere, no "pro tier" hiding the good features.
- **An AI agent platform.** Agents are a capability inside NodeTool. They're not the product.

## 6. Personality

Six adjectives, each with a guardrail:

- **Crafted, not corporate.** We ship polish; we don't ship press releases.
- **Open, not naive.** Open source as conviction, not as a hobby project — we run a real product, not a GitHub experiment.
- **Direct, not edgy.** We say what we mean. We don't perform irreverence to seem cool.
- **Technical, not jargon-y.** We respect that our users are smart. We don't hide behind acronyms.
- **Generous, not preachy.** We share workflows, templates, and credit freely. We don't lecture about ethics or "the future of creativity."
- **Confident, not adversarial.** We name competitors honestly. We don't trash them.

## 7. Voice

Five rules anyone on the team can apply without checking with anyone:

1. **Write to a working creative, not their manager.** Sentences should sound like Discord, not LinkedIn.
2. **Lead with the artifact.** Show the image, the video, the workflow first. Explain second.
3. **Banned words: empower, unleash, revolutionize, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changing, AI-powered.** Replace with what's actually happening.
4. **Name things by their real names.** Flux is Flux. Seedance is Seedance. Inpainting is inpainting. Don't invent jargon to sound sophisticated.
5. **Active voice, present tense.** "NodeTool runs your models locally." Not "Models can be run locally using NodeTool."

## Messaging hierarchy

**Tagline:** *The open creative AI workspace.*

**Sub-headline / differentiator:** *Every model. Your keys. Your canvas.*

**One-sentence pitch:**
NodeTool is the open-source creative AI workspace — every major model from every major provider, called with your own keys, wired into one node-based canvas you run on your machine.

**One-paragraph pitch:**
Creative pros working with AI are stuck choosing between ComfyUI's engineer-first UX, closed SaaS canvases — like Weavy, now part of Figma — that lock you into a credit system and a curated model roster, or a dozen browser tabs across Midjourney, Runway, and Photoshop. NodeTool is the open alternative. Bring your own keys to FAL, KIE, Atlas, OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Replicate — pay providers directly at provider prices, and switch the moment a better model ships. Wire it all into one node-based canvas with the editing tools you rely on: masks, inpaint, outpaint, relight, upscale, layers, compositing. Runs as a desktop app on your Mac, PC, or Linux box, or in the browser via NodeTool Cloud. Local inference when you want it. No credit markup, no vendor lock-in, no acquisition risk.

**The proof story (use this everywhere):**
Seedance is the best video model right now. It's available on FAL, Replicate, and KIE at different price points. NodeTool lets you pick the cheapest. When Veo 4 ships, you swap one node and you're on it the same day. That's what vendor neutrality actually buys you.

## Vocabulary

**Use:** workspace, canvas, creative AI, open source, BYOK, your keys, provider prices, model-agnostic, transparent pricing, vendor-neutral, the best model for the job, switch providers, runs on your machine, your model library, workflow, node, craft.

**Don't use:** orchestration, agentic, LLM (when you mean "model"), no-code, low-code, platform (alone), enterprise (in headlines), seamless, empower, unleash, AI-powered, credits (as currency), tokens (as currency), AI hosting, managed inference.

**Be careful with:**
- **"agent"** — only on the agents page, not the home page.
- **"local"** — a feature, not the headline. The headline is openness and vendor neutrality.
- **"developer"** — developer audience exists but isn't the wedge.
- **"automation"** — sounds like n8n.
